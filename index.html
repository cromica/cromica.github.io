ABOUT ME

I’m a software developer. I’m a trainer. I’m a speaker. I’m a metalhead. I’m a football player. I’m a husband and father. I’m Agile.

Passion

I’m passionate about writing code, mentoring and coaching. After I graduated I wasn’t really sure if this is what I want to do so I’ve tried different things but didn’t like what I was doing. I’ve realized during this period that I miss writing code and learning new technologies. Today I love what I’m doing and for me is not just a job it's a hobby, a way of living.

Challenge

I embrace challenges because they make me a better person. Challenges get us out of our comfort zone. Getting out of our comfort zone means that we are learning new things, that we are growing. Learning and mastering new things is one of the most rewarding things that can happen. As software developers we need to face all kinds of challenges like rapid change of technology, build software tailored to the customer needs, deal with abstractions and patterns, be a software craftsman and lots of other things.

People

I love to share my knowledge and experience with other people. It’s always nice to share your ideas with someone and get some feedback. I also think that in order to be successful you need to grow the right people which is not the easiest thing to do but is quite rewarding. Offering mentoring, coaching and training is probably the best way to learn.

Software Development

I love writing code and I do it for quite a while. Most of the time I write code in .NET using most of the platform frameworks and SDK’s like ASP.NET MVC| ASP.NET Wep Api| Windows Azure| ASP.NET WebForms| WCF| Entity Frameworkand many others. I also have good experience with Oracle PL/SQLand MS SQL Serverbut I don’t necessarily consider myself a database developer. Recently I’ve done some personal projects using AngularJS| BreezeJSand some other SPA libraries. I’ve worked on all kinds projects with teams between 2 and 30 people on various domains like financial, digital media and ecommerce.

Enterprise Architecture

During the last years I’ve been working for a big enterprise company helping them integratevarious systems in their ecosystem. Working with global companiesmeans that you need to integrate systems around the globe using different technologies and data centers. To accomplish this we’ve combined their on premise infrastructure with cloud platforms like Microsoft Azure. This constantly pushed me out of my comfort zonewhich is great because I grewa lot.

Mentoring & Coaching

Part of being a senior developer means that it’s your responsibility to mentor and coach juniors in their careers. In the last years I’ve mentor and coach more than 30 peoplejuniors and seniors in various areas like leadership, scrum, software developmentand people management. Working with people is never easy but in the same time is very rewarding when you see them grew.

Speaking

Public speaking is toughbecause to be in front of many people is intimidating. The filling is quite similar regardless if you are at a conference, in front of your team or your client. In the same time is one the best way to present or share your ideas| knowledge| experienceand get useful feedback. In the last years I held presentations to my colleagues, clientsand conferences.

Training

Technology change at a rapid pacewhich there is a constant need of training. Provide trainings is one of the greatest way to learn and know other people. I’ve done various trainings on .NET technologies and other topic like WCF| ASP.NET MVC| ASP.NET Webforms MVP| Dependency Injection| TDD.
